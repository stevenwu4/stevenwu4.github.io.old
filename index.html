About Me

  • Joining MDA's R&D team starting Summer 2016 on an 8 month co-op term, combining programming and research to work on problems involving spatial statistics and image/video data

  • Undergoing my MSc Statistics @ Simon Fraser University under the supervision of fellow sports junkie Dr. Tim Swartz, with funding through the NSERC CGS-M grant.

  • Creator of SeeCIS, implemented using Python/R/MongoDB, to leverage CIS (Canadian University league) basketball play-by-play data. In its state, analysis can't be performed mostly because substitutions are recorded incorrectly. How can you calculate a player's +/- if you're not sure he's in the game or not? After seeing one too many instances of a player who "enters the game" twice, I developed algorithms to automatically clean a game, and most importantly, guarantee 10 players on the court at all times. I've also developed metrics that justify the changes I make to the games.

  • Graduated B. Mathematics, Computational and Applied Mathematics and Statistics degree @ Carleton University. Interesting classes taken include: Data Mining I, Spatial Statistics, Time Series Analysis, Artificial Intelligence, Systems Programming, Software Engineering.

  • Just finished working as a Technical Services Developer @ 360pi - writing new crawlers, updating old ones, and making improvements to the infrastructure. On previous co-op terms I started with manual quality assurance and ended working on test automation. Almost everything that I have learned and know about Python and programming is thanks to the inspiration and advice of my very smart and very friendly coworkers.

  • I like helping people out when I can. I have been a teaching assistant for 15 different 1st year/2nd year mathematics/statistics classes. I've worked as a Co-op Peer Helper on a voluntary basis for a year and I also worked as a Campus Tour Guide for a year. Organizing CUMC 2014 was way more work than we all expected.

  • I'm currently employed at the Centre for Quantitative Analysis and Decision Support at Carleton University writing blog articles for the launch of the new website, cqads.carleton.ca.
